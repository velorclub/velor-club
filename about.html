What we believe

The best relationships
start with a shared activity,
not a shared feed.

Social media made it easier to connect with people and harder to actually spend time with them. We're trying to reverse that — to build a platform that ends in a hike or a workshop or a campfire, not in another hour of scrolling.

Our model is simple: communities organized around activities, not identities. Events that get people in a room (or on a trail) together. And a platform that gets out of the way once you've found your people.

We're starting in Nepal because it's home — and because there's no better place to test whether people genuinely want to do things together. Turns out they really do.

How we operate

What we stand for

🏔

Real over virtual

Every feature we build is designed to end with people in the same physical place, not in more time on their phones.

🤝

Member-led communities

Groups are owned and run by the people in them. Velor provides the infrastructure — the community provides the soul.

🌏

Local first, global vision

We build for the specific context of the cities we're in — then find what generalizes. No generic global product that fits nowhere.

✨

Quality over quantity

A hundred great events beats a thousand mediocre ones. We'd rather have fewer, better communities than flood the platform.

🔒

Safe spaces

Verified organizers, clear community guidelines, and a reporting system that actually gets used. Trust is not optional.

🚀

Ship and iterate

We move fast, listen closely, and update the platform based on what members actually use — not what we assumed they'd want.
